BD475-2 CRAWLER DOZER

The BD475-2 is a powerful dozer with a fuel-efficient Cummins QST30 T2 Tier-II engine and smooth joystick-based control. It delivers strong traction for dozing and ripping through stress-absorbing undercarriages. The low-profile design with a panoramic, noise-suppressed ROPS-FOPS cabin ensures operator comfort and stability. Its modular construction and advanced monitoring system support easier maintenance and reliable performance in harsh environments.

Engine

QST30 T2 Tier-II

The BD475-2 is powered by the Cummins QST30 T2 Tier-II electronic engine which provides maximum fuel conservation. Smooth and responsive gear shifts with a joystick allow instant speed and directional changes. It delivers 634 kW (850 HP) @ 2100 rpm and has an operating mass of 100,000 kg with ripper. Conveniently located armchair steering control enhances operator comfort inside the ROPS-FOPS hexagonal sound-suppressive cabin.

Stress-absorbing undercarriages offer mighty traction for powerful dozing and ripping operations. The automatic lock-up torque converter maximizes production while ensuring excellent fuel economy. The electronic engine ensures fuel-efficient performance throughout heavy workloads. Undercarriage design helps extend service life during continuous operations.

A unique modular design allows easy removal of powertrain components without oil spillage. The advanced monitoring system helps prevent minor issues from turning into major problems. The machine’s extra-low profile ensures excellent dynamic stability during operation. A large cab window area provides a panoramic view and the well-sealed cabin ensures noise suppression.

Joystick-based blade control offers effortless and precise handling for dozing applications. The operator-focused cabin layout provides comfort and reduces fatigue over long working hours. Smooth and responsive controls improve operational ease and productivity. Overall, the BD475-2 is engineered to deliver strong performance, operator comfort, and durability in demanding earthmoving environments.
